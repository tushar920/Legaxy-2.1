NRI Services

Managing Wealth Across Borders.

Managing finances across countries comes with unique challenges. Regulations, taxation, repatriation rules, and investment restrictions make financial planning for Non Resident Indians more complex than for resident investors.

At STRYD, we help NRIs navigate Indian investments with clarity and confidence. Our NRI services are designed to simplify investing in India while ensuring compliance with regulatory guidelines and alignment with your long-term financial goals.

Foreign investments in India are governed by policies issued by the Government of India and regulated under FEMA. These policies allow NRIs and Persons of Indian Origin to invest in a wide range of financial instruments under permitted routes, subject to applicable rules. STRYD assists you in understanding these regulations and structuring your investments accordingly.

Our NRI Advisory Approach

We provide structured guidance to NRIs looking to build, manage, and preserve wealth in India. Our advisory focuses on combining regulatory compliance with goal-based investment planning.

  • Investment planning aligned with your global income and Indian goals.
  • Guidance on repatriable and non-repatriable investment options.
  • Mutual fund and fixed income investment advisory.
  • Tax-efficient investment structuring.
  • Long-term wealth creation and portfolio monitoring.
  • Digital access to portfolio tracking and reporting.

We ensure your Indian investments remain simple, transparent, and aligned with your financial priorities.

Who is Considered an NRI

A Non Resident Indian is an Indian citizen who stays outside India for employment, business, or any purpose that indicates an indefinite period of stay abroad. This includes:

  • Indian citizens working abroad with foreign governments or international organizations.
  • Individuals deputed overseas by Indian government bodies or public sector undertakings.

An NRI becomes a resident in India only when returning for employment, business, or any purpose indicating long-term stay. Short visits for holidays or personal reasons do not change residential status.

Persons of Indian Origin

For investment and banking purposes, a foreign citizen is treated as a Person of Indian Origin if:

  • They previously held an Indian passport.
  • Either parent or grandparent was a citizen of India under the Constitution or Citizenship Act.
  • The spouse of an Indian citizen or Person of Indian Origin, subject to permitted joint investment rules.

STRYD assists both NRIs and Persons of Indian Origin in understanding eligibility and structuring investments accordingly.

Estate Planning

Protecting Your Legacy.

Estate planning is the process of ensuring a smooth and intentional transfer of wealth to the next generation. It allows you to decide how your assets should be distributed and helps protect your loved ones from uncertainty, conflict, and legal complications.

At STRYD, estate planning focuses on clarity, control, and continuity. A well-structured estate plan ensures that your wealth passes into the right hands according to your wishes while keeping your family financially secure. Writing a clear and thoughtful Will is a critical part of estate planning.

Why Estate Planning is Important
  • Clearly define beneficiaries and asset distribution.
  • Protect family members from legal and financial uncertainty.
  • Ensure continuity of wealth across generations.
  • Minimize disputes among heirs.
  • Maintain control over how and when assets are transferred.
Important Points to Remember
  • Nomination in financial products does not automatically transfer ownership.
  • A nominee is not the same as a legal heir.
  • Appointing an executor ensures proper execution of your Will.
  • A registered Will significantly reduces disputes.
  • A Will can be modified at any time.
STRYD's Approach to Estate Planning
  • Guidance on drafting and structuring a Will.
  • Reviewing asset ownership and beneficiary details.
  • Alignment of nominations with estate objectives.
  • Coordination with tax and financial planning.
  • Long-term planning for family and legacy needs.
